# Copy Review Checklist
> Use this before publishing any piece of copy — social post, ad, email, landing page, or caption.

---

## Section 1 — The Headline / Hook

- [ ] Does the headline deliver a specific, compelling benefit (not a feature, not a vague tease)?
- [ ] Would your ideal reader stop scrolling for this?
- [ ] Does it score 3 or 4 on at least 3 of the 4 U's (Urgent, Unique, Ultra-specific, Useful)?
- [ ] Is there a clear audience signal — does the right person immediately know it's for them?
- [ ] Is it 15 words or fewer (for most platforms)?
- [ ] Does it stand alone as a complete thought (vital for Instagram/LinkedIn previews)?

---

## Section 2 — The Body Copy

- [ ] Does the first sentence immediately follow through on the headline's promise?
- [ ] Is the copy you-oriented — "you," "your," "you'll" used throughout?
- [ ] Have you translated every feature into a tangible benefit?
- [ ] Is the copy specific? (Replace "saves time" → "saves 47 minutes per day")
- [ ] Is the copy concise? Read it once and cut every word that doesn't earn its place.
- [ ] Are sentences short (avg 14–16 words; break anything over 30)?
- [ ] Are paragraphs short (1–3 lines on digital; 1 line on mobile)?
- [ ] Is the copy believable? Is every claim backed by proof (data, testimonial, demonstration)?
- [ ] Does it flow naturally when read aloud? No awkward phrases, no jarring transitions?
- [ ] Is the problem clearly articulated before the solution is introduced?
- [ ] If using a formula (AIDA, PAS, Motivating Sequence) — have all steps been hit?

---

## Section 3 — Proof & Credibility

- [ ] Is there at least one form of proof? (Testimonial / data / case study / guarantee / endorsement)
- [ ] Are testimonials specific? ("It changed my life" = weak. "I closed 3 clients in one week" = strong)
- [ ] If making a bold claim, is there a secondary promise for skeptical readers?
- [ ] Are statistics sourced or at least named? ("Studies show" < "A 2024 MIT study found")
- [ ] Does the copy avoid hype words without backing? (amazing, incredible, best-ever — unless proven)

---

## Section 4 — The Call to Action

- [ ] Is there exactly ONE clear CTA — not two, not zero?
- [ ] Does the CTA tell the reader specifically what to do? ("Click here" < "Download the free guide")
- [ ] Is there a reason to act NOW (urgency or immediate benefit)?
- [ ] Is the CTA frictionless — does it feel easy, low-risk, or free?
- [ ] Is the CTA visually distinct or at least on its own line?

---

## Section 5 — Platform Compliance

- [ ] Does the copy fit within the platform's character limits?
- [ ] Does it comply with the platform's advertising guidelines (weight loss, financial claims, etc.)?
- [ ] Is the image text under 20% of image area (Facebook/Instagram ads)?
- [ ] Are links formatted correctly for the platform (no links in Instagram captions, etc.)?
- [ ] Are hashtags relevant and limited to the sweet spot (3–10 for most platforms)?

---

## Section 6 — The Big Picture

- [ ] What is the single strongest takeaway a reader gets from this copy?
  > Write it in one sentence: "After reading this, my audience will believe/feel/do ___."
- [ ] Does every sentence serve that single purpose? Cut anything that doesn't.
- [ ] Is this copy aligned with the BFD of the audience? (Their Beliefs, Feelings, Desires)
- [ ] Would your ideal customer read this and think "This is exactly what I needed to hear"?

---

## Quick 60-Second Pre-Publish Check

Read through your copy and ask:

| Question | Pass / Fail |
|---|---|
| Would I stop scrolling for this headline? | |
| Does the first sentence earn its place? | |
| Is there one clear benefit in the first 2 lines? | |
| Is there at least one form of proof? | |
| Is there one clear CTA? | |
| Did I cut at least 20% of my first draft? | |
| Is it written to ONE specific person? | |

If any row is "Fail" — fix it before publishing.

## Platform-Specific Mini-Checklists

### LinkedIn Post
- [ ] First line works as a standalone hook (under 210 chars)
- [ ] No link in the post body (put in comments)
- [ ] Ends with a question or soft CTA
- [ ] 3–5 relevant hashtags max

### Twitter/X Thread
- [ ] First tweet works standalone and hints at the full value
- [ ] Each tweet = one idea
- [ ] Final tweet has CTA + ask for retweet/follow
- [ ] Under 280 chars per tweet

### Instagram Caption
- [ ] First line stops the scroll (under 125 chars before "more")
- [ ] Body has clear value (story, tips, or insight)
- [ ] CTA is specific (save, share, comment, click link in bio)
- [ ] Hashtags at end or in first comment

### Facebook Ad
- [ ] Primary text: states the offer, benefit, and CTA in under 125 chars
- [ ] Headline: under 25 characters, includes a number or benefit
- [ ] Does NOT make claims Facebook will reject
- [ ] Image has minimal text

### Email Subject Line
- [ ] Under 50 characters
- [ ] Rated 3+/4 on at least 3 of the 4 U's
- [ ] Passes the "would I open this?" test
- [ ] Preview text (snippet) adds to — not repeats — the subject line

## The Final Edit: Cut List

In your final edit, delete or replace:

| Cut This | Replace With |
|---|---|
| "In today's world…" | Start with the problem directly |
| "I'm excited to share…" | Share it — skip the announcement |
| "As we all know…" | State the insight; don't tell them they know it |
| "Synergy / leverage / utilize" | Work together / use / use |
| "Very / really / extremely" | Delete or use a stronger word |
| "I think / I believe / In my opinion" | Just state it — hedging weakens copy |
| "At the end of the day" | Delete entirely |
| "It goes without saying" | Then don't say it |
| "In order to" | Replace with "to" |
| Any sentence over 35 words | Break into two |

---

## The Most Important Rule

> **"Copywriters are salespeople whose job is to convince people to buy products."** — Robert W. Bly

Every edit you make should push the copy closer to a sale, a click, a sign-up, or an action. Beauty is a bonus. Results are the goal.
