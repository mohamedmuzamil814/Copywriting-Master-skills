# Master Copywriting Skill
> Distilled from Robert W. Bly — *The Copywriter's Handbook* (3rd & 4th Editions)
> Applicable to every social media platform and digital marketing channel.

## Core Philosophy
**"A copywriter is a salesperson behind a keyboard."** — Judith Charles

Copy has one job: **sell**. Not entertain. Not win awards. Not impress. Every word must move the reader closer to action. Great copy is invisible — the reader only sees the product and its benefit.

Three forces govern modern copy:
1. **Human Emotion** — the primary driver of all buying decisions
2. **Data** — what analytics prove works vs. what feels clever
3. **Compliance** — platform rules that constrain how boldly you can write

> "People buy based on emotion, then rationalize the purchase decision with logic." — Michael Masterson

---

## Step 1 — Before You Write: Research the BFD

Before writing a single word, map the audience's **BFD (Beliefs, Feelings, Desires)**:

| Dimension | Question to Answer |
|---|---|
| **Beliefs** | What does your audience already believe about the problem and your category? |
| **Feelings** | How do they feel right now — frustrated, hopeful, skeptical, urgent? |
| **Desires** | What outcome do they want? What change are they hoping for? |

Then identify **copy motivators** — the real reasons people buy:
- To make money / save money / save time
- To be liked, appreciated, important, attractive, sexy
- To feel secure, comfortable, healthy, happy
- Out of fear, greed, guilt, or curiosity
- To gain knowledge or have fun

Pick 1–2 dominant motivators and write directly to them.

---

## Step 2 — The USP (Unique Selling Proposition)

Every piece of copy needs a USP. Three requirements (Rosser Reeves):
1. **Make a specific promise** — "Buy this and get *this exact benefit*"
2. **Be unique** — the proposition must differentiate from every competitor
3. **Be strong enough to move people** — trivial differences don't work

If your product has no unique feature, find a benefit the competition ignores, dramatize a known benefit compellingly, or stake your claim on it before anyone else does.

## Step 3 — The Headline / Hook

**On average, 5× more people read the headline than the body copy. Spend 80% of your energy here.**

### The 4 U's Formula (rate each 1–4; aim for 3+ on at least 3 categories)
1. **Urgent** — gives the reader a reason to act *now*, not later
2. **Unique** — says something new, or an old thing in a fresh way
3. **Ultra-specific** — uses numbers, names, or precise details ("Save 43% by Tuesday")
4. **Useful** — promises a clear benefit that the reader actually wants

### Four Functions of a Headline
1. Get attention
2. Select the audience (who this is for)
3. Deliver a complete message
4. Pull the reader into the body copy

### Proven Headline Patterns
- **Self-interest**: "How to Win Friends and Influence People"
- **News**: Use words like *new, announcing, introducing, now, at last*
- **Free**: "Free Report Reveals..."
- **How-to**: "How to [Desired Result] Without [Feared Cost]"
- **Question**: "Know the Secret to [Desired Outcome]?"
- **Number**: "7 Ways to [Solve Problem] This [Time Period]"
- **Command**: "Stop [Pain]. Start [Gain]."
- **Curiosity gap**: Be specific about the problem, vague and intriguing about the solution

### Power Words
`free · how to · you · save · results · proven · guarantee · new · discover · easy · quick · last chance · bargain`

> **Never** use a pun, clever wordplay, or vague teaser as your headline unless it directly connects to a strong benefit. Clever ≠ converting.

## Step 4 — The Body Copy Structure (Motivating Sequence)

Use this five-step sequence for any post, ad, or page:

1. **Get Attention** — headline/hook delivers the strongest benefit
2. **Show a Need** — agitate the problem; make the reader feel it
3. **Satisfy the Need** — position your product/service/idea as the solution
4. **Prove It** — testimonials, data, case studies, specifics, demonstrations
5. **Ask for Action** — clear, specific CTA with zero ambiguity

**Other proven formulas** (load `references/formulas.md` for full details):
- **AIDA** — Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
- **4 P's** — Picture → Promise → Prove → Push
- **PAS** — Problem → Agitate → Solution
- **ACCA** — Awareness → Comprehension → Conviction → Action

## Step 5 — Writing Clarity Rules (The 4 S Formula)

**Short Words. Short Sentences. Short Paragraphs. Short Sections.**

| Rule | Standard |
|---|---|
| Sentence length | 14–16 words average; max ~34 before readability drops |
| Paragraphs | 2–4 lines in digital; 1–2 on mobile |
| Words | Replace big words with simple ones (use → use, not utilize) |
| Sections | Break at every new idea |

**11 Clarity Tips**
1. Write to the reader as "you" — you-orientation over advertiser-orientation
2. Organize selling points by importance: strongest first
3. Break copy into short sections with subheads
4. Use short sentences; vary length to create rhythm
5. Use small, simple words
6. Avoid jargon unless 95%+ of your audience knows it
7. Be concise — cut every word that doesn't earn its place
8. Be specific — "saves 43 minutes a day" beats "saves time"
9. Write conversationally — read it aloud; if it sounds stiff, rewrite
10. Make it believable — back up claims with proof
11. Flow smoothly — no jarring phrases; one thought leads naturally to the next

---

## Step 6 — The Call to Action (CTA)

Every piece of copy must end with a CTA. Make it:
- **Specific** — "Click here" < "Download the free guide" < "Get your free 7-day meal plan"
- **Low-friction** — reduce the perceived effort/risk
- **Urgent** — create a reason to act now (limited time, limited quantity, immediate benefit)
- **Single** — one CTA per post/ad; two choices = no choice

## Step 7 — Platform Adaptation

See `references/platforms.md` for full specs, character limits, and examples. Summary:

| Platform | Tone | Length | Hook Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | Professional, authoritative, story-driven | 150–300 words (posts); 25-char headline (ads) | Lead with insight or personal story |
| **Twitter / X** | Punchy, opinionated, one idea | 280 chars; thread for depth | First tweet = standalone hook |
| **Instagram** | Visual-first, emotion-led, lifestyle | Caption: 125–150 chars visible; 2,200 max | First line before "more" is the headline |
| **Facebook** | Conversational, benefit-forward | 40–80 words (feed); 125 chars (ads) | Lead with problem or curiosity |
| **TikTok** | Raw, authentic, fast | Hook in first 2 seconds (text overlay) | Question or bold statement |
| **YouTube** | Title-driven SEO + curiosity gap | Title: 60 chars; description: keyword-rich | Number + benefit in title |
| **Pinterest** | Aspirational, how-to, visually described | 100–200 chars | "How to" or aspirational outcome |
| **Reddit** | Community-first, value-heavy, no-sell | Long-form; proof-led | Teach first, mention product last |

---

## Step 8 — Review Checklist

Before publishing any copy, ask:
- [ ] Does the headline deliver a clear, compelling benefit?
- [ ] Does the copy fulfill the promise the headline made?
- [ ] Is every sentence you-oriented (benefit to reader, not description of you)?
- [ ] Have you translated features into benefits?
- [ ] Is the copy specific — facts, numbers, proof?
- [ ] Is it concise — zero unnecessary words?
- [ ] Is it believable — backed by evidence or social proof?
- [ ] Does it flow naturally when read aloud?
- [ ] Is there one clear CTA at the end?
- [ ] Does it comply with platform rules?
