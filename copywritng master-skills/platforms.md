# Platform-Specific Copywriting Guide
> Specs, tone guidelines, formulas, and examples for every major social media platform

---

## Universal Rule
Write differently for each platform, even if the underlying topic is the same.
**Same message. Different format. Different hook. Different length.**

---

## LinkedIn

**Audience**: Professionals, B2B buyers, job seekers, thought leaders
**Goal**: Establish authority, generate leads, build professional relationships
**Tone**: Authoritative, insightful, occasionally vulnerable, always substantive

### Copy Specs (Organic Posts)
- Visible before "see more": ~210 characters (mobile), ~3 lines
- Max length: 3,000 characters
- Ideal length: 150–300 words for engagement
- No clickable links in post body (put in comments for reach)
- Use line breaks aggressively — every 1–2 sentences

### Copy Specs (LinkedIn Ads)
- Headline: 25 characters max
- Description: 75 characters max
- Sponsored Content intro text: 150 characters ideal
- InMail subject: 60 characters; body: 500–1,000 words

### Hook Patterns (First Line is Everything)
- "I made a $50,000 mistake. Here's what I learned."
- "Nobody tells you this about [industry topic]."
- "After [X years] doing [thing], here's the only framework that works."
- "Controversial opinion: [bold claim]"
- "The most underrated [skill/strategy] in [field]:"
- Data hook: "73% of [audience] fail at [X]. Here's why."

### Post Structure
1. Hook (1 line — bold, specific, provokes curiosity or nods)
2. Story or insight (3–7 short paragraphs, each 1–2 lines)
3. Takeaway / lesson / reframe
4. CTA (soft: "What do you think?" or direct: "DM me for the template")

### LinkedIn Dos & Don'ts
✅ Tell a specific story, not a vague lesson
✅ Use "I" and first person — authenticity wins
✅ Add a hook line that works as a standalone sentence
✅ End with engagement bait (question) or soft CTA
❌ Don't put URLs in the body (kills reach)
❌ Don't use hashtag spam (3–5 relevant max)
❌ Don't write dense paragraphs — use white space

## Twitter / X

**Audience**: Diverse — tech, media, finance, politics, creators
**Goal**: Build following, establish a POV, drive traffic, spark conversation
**Tone**: Punchy, opinionated, conversational, confident

### Copy Specs
- Single tweet: 280 characters
- Thread: 280 per tweet; 3–25 tweets is a typical thread
- No character limit on Spaces or long-form Notes

### Hook Patterns (Standalone Tweets)
- Contrarian opinion: "Hot take: [Widely believed thing] is actually wrong."
- Curiosity gap: "Most people get [X] backwards. The real answer is ☟"
- Simple value: "5 things I wish I knew before [experience]:"
- Bold claim: "The single best [productivity/marketing/business] advice:"
- Story starter: "Thread: How I went from [state A] to [state B] ↓"

### Thread Structure
**Tweet 1** = The hook. Must work as standalone. States the payoff.
> "I studied 100 viral posts. Here's the exact formula behind all of them: 🧵"

**Tweets 2–N** = Deliver the value. One idea per tweet. Short.

**Final Tweet** = Summary + CTA
> "TL;DR: [3-word summary]. Follow @[handle] for more. Retweet if this helped."

### Twitter Dos & Don'ts
✅ One idea per tweet — no compound sentences in threads
✅ Use numbers ("3 reasons," "7 steps")
✅ End threads with a clear CTA + ask for RT
✅ Reply to comments in first hour for algorithm boost
❌ Don't put the main point in replies — front-load it
❌ Don't thread what can be said in one tweet
❌ Avoid vague "thoughts?" — ask a specific question

## Instagram

**Audience**: Broad — lifestyle, fashion, food, fitness, creators, brands
**Goal**: Build community, showcase brand, sell products, drive profile traffic
**Tone**: Visual-first, emotion-led, aspirational, authentic

### Copy Specs
- Caption visible before "more": ~125 characters (approximately 2 lines)
- Full caption max: 2,200 characters
- Hashtags: up to 30; sweet spot is 5–10 relevant ones
- Story text overlay: Keep to 1–2 short sentences max
- Bio: 150 characters

### Caption Structure
**Line 1** = The hook (this is your headline — must work before the "more" cut)
> "Nobody warned me about this part of starting a business."
> "3 signs your content strategy is broken 👇"
> "This one change doubled my engagement in a week."

**Lines 2–8** = Body — story, insight, or value delivery. Short paragraphs, emojis as separators.

**CTA line** = Direct action
> "Save this for later 📌" / "Tag someone who needs this" / "Link in bio to get the full guide"

**Hashtags** = Either in caption (after a few line breaks) or in first comment

### Instagram Dos & Don'ts
✅ First line must stop the scroll — test it as a standalone sentence
✅ Use storytelling — start with "I" or "You" + a specific moment
✅ Emojis as visual bullets/separators work well
✅ CTA should match your goal (save, share, click, comment)
❌ Don't waste line 1 with "Good morning!" or your brand name
❌ Don't stuff hashtags in the visible part of the caption
❌ Don't use clickable links in captions (they don't work — use bio link)

### Instagram Ad Copy
- Primary text: 125 characters (shown before truncation)
- Headline: 25 characters
- Description: 30 characters
- Image: minimal text (less than 20% of image area)
- Formula: What you offer + How it benefits them + What to do next

## Facebook

**Audience**: Broad — 25–65+, diverse interests, community-oriented
**Goal**: Engagement, lead generation, local awareness, sales
**Tone**: Conversational, warm, community-minded, relatable

### Copy Specs (Organic)
- Ideal length for engagement: 40–80 words
- Posts over 480 characters get truncated
- Links: Always add a preview image (Facebook generates it from URL)

### Copy Specs (Facebook Ads)
- Primary text: 125 characters ideal (more is allowed but truncated on mobile)
- Headline: 25 characters
- Description (link): 30 characters
- Image: Minimal text; 20% rule still applies in spirit
- Video caption: 2,200 characters max

### AdEspresso's 8 Rules for Facebook Ad Headlines
1. Use numbers
2. Create a sense of urgency
3. Be clear and precise
4. Keep headlines short
5. Emphasize the benefits
6. Include a call to action
7. Ask a question
8. Use powerful words

### Facebook Ad Formula (WordStream)
Tell the reader 3 things:
1. **What you're offering**
2. **How it benefits them**
3. **What to do next**

### Facebook Dos & Don'ts
✅ Lead with a question or relatable statement
✅ Write like you're talking to one person
✅ Add social proof ("Over 12,000 people have used this…")
✅ Use "you" frequently
❌ Don't use corporate language — Facebook is a social space
❌ Don't make promises Facebook's compliance team will reject (weight loss specifics, etc.)
❌ Avoid clickbait — Facebook actively suppresses it

## TikTok

**Audience**: Gen Z primary, Millennials secondary; entertainment + education hybrids
**Goal**: Reach, virality, awareness, product discovery, community building
**Tone**: Raw, authentic, fast-paced, entertaining, educational

### Copy Specs
- Caption: up to 2,200 characters; most users read first 100
- On-screen text overlay: 1–2 sentences; high contrast; large font
- Hook window: first 2–3 seconds (video) = your entire headline

### Hook Patterns (Video + Caption)
- POV opener: "POV: You just discovered [X]"
- Curiosity: "I can't believe this actually works."
- Story hook: "This mistake cost me $10,000."
- Pattern interrupt: "Nobody is talking about this."
- Education: "3 things [niche audience] does differently:"

### TikTok Dos & Don'ts
✅ The hook IS the copy — spend 80% of effort on first 3 seconds
✅ On-screen text should summarize what's being said (for mute viewers)
✅ Use trending audio to increase reach
✅ Keep CTAs simple: "Follow for part 2" / "Comment [word] for my free guide"
❌ Don't look over-produced — raw authenticity outperforms polished ads
❌ Don't delay the hook — no long intros

## YouTube

**Audience**: All ages; intent-based (people searching for specific info)
**Goal**: Views, subscribers, brand authority, long-form sales
**Tone**: Educational, personality-driven, value-first

### Copy Specs
- Video title: 60 characters max (before truncation)
- Description: First 2–3 lines visible before "Show more"; max 5,000 chars
- Tags: 500 characters total
- Community posts: 1,080 characters

### Title Formulas
- Number + Benefit: "7 Copywriting Secrets That 10x'd My Revenue"
- Curiosity gap: "The Real Reason Your Copy Isn't Converting (It's Not What You Think)"
- Tutorial: "How to Write a LinkedIn Post That Gets 100K Views (Step-by-Step)"
- Versus: "Long-Form vs Short-Form Copy: Which Wins in 2025?"
- Story: "I Rewrote My Homepage Copy — Here's What Happened"

### YouTube Dos & Don'ts
✅ Keyword research first — match what people search
✅ First line of description = primary keyword + hook
✅ Pin a comment with a CTA/link
❌ Don't frontload the description with timestamps — lose keyword value
❌ Don't use vague titles like "Episode 47"

## Pinterest

**Audience**: Primarily women 25–45; high purchase intent; planning mindset
**Goal**: Traffic, product discovery, saves, brand awareness
**Tone**: Aspirational, instructional, visual-led

### Copy Specs
- Pin title: 100 characters (first 30–40 most visible)
- Pin description: 500 characters max; 100–200 sweet spot
- Board title: 50 characters
- Profile bio: 160 characters

### Title & Description Patterns
- "How to [Achieve Outcome] in [Time Frame]"
- "The Ultimate [Category] Guide for [Audience]"
- "[Number] [Category] Ideas for [Context/Occasion]"
- "Easy [Adjective] [Topic] You Can Do Today"

## Reddit

**Audience**: Highly knowledgeable, deeply skeptical of marketing, community-first
**Goal**: Brand awareness, organic reach, genuine engagement
**Tone**: Conversational, helpful, transparent — anti-sales

### Copy Rules
1. **Provide genuine value first** — teach, share, help. Mention product/brand last or not at all.
2. **Match the subreddit's style** — read 20 posts before writing one
3. **Disclose commercial interest** always — Reddit users will find it; transparency protects you
4. **Long-form wins in most subreddits** — depth = credibility
5. **No CTAs in post body** — if linking, be transparent about what it is

## Threads / Bluesky / Emerging Platforms

Apply the Twitter/X framework:
- Hook-first, one idea per post
- Thread for depth
- Conversational tone
- Engage back in comments

## Cross-Platform Adaptation Example

Same content, adapted for each platform:

**Core message**: A new productivity framework that helps founders save 10 hours/week

| Platform | Copy |
|---|---|
| **LinkedIn** | "I was working 70-hour weeks and still missing deadlines. Then I found one framework that cut my work to 55 hours without dropping a single client. Here's exactly how it works:" + 200-word breakdown |
| **Twitter** | "Thread: The exact productivity system that saved me 10 hours/week as a solo founder ↓" |
| **Instagram** | "This framework saved me 10 hours a week 👇 (swipe to see the full system)" |
| **Facebook** | "Be honest — how many hours are you actually wasting on things that don't move the needle? Here's the system I use that got me back 10 hours every week:" |
| **TikTok** | Hook (text overlay): "I work 10 fewer hours and make more money. Here's why." |
| **YouTube** | Title: "The 4-Block System That Saves Founders 10 Hours Every Week" |
