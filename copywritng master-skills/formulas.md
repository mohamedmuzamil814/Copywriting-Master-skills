# Copy Formulas Reference
> All major copywriting formulas from Bly's Copywriter's Handbook

---

## 1. The Motivating Sequence (Bly's Preferred)

The most complete formula for long-form copy, landing pages, and sales posts.

**Step 1 — Get Attention**
- Headline/hook = the single strongest benefit
- Don't save the best for last; lead with your biggest gun
- The reader must think: "This is for me"

**Step 2 — Show a Need**
- Agitate the problem the reader already has
- Make them feel the pain of NOT acting
- Ask: "What keeps them up at night?"

**Step 3 — Satisfy the Need (Position Your Solution)**
- Show your product/idea/service solves the exact problem
- Be specific: "Here's exactly how it works"
- Connect features → benefits immediately

**Step 4 — Prove It**
Proof types in order of persuasive power:
1. Customer testimonials (specific results, real names)
2. Case studies with numbers
3. Data/research/clinical studies
4. Demonstrations or visual proof
5. Social proof (number of users, press mentions)
6. Guarantees and risk reversals
7. Expert endorsements
8. Awards or certifications

**Step 5 — Ask for Action**
- Tell the reader exactly what to do next
- One action only — one link, one button, one next step
- Create urgency: deadline, scarcity, immediate benefit

## 2. AIDA

Classic formula. Works for emails, ads, posts, pitches.

- **A — Attention**: Hook with headline or opening line
- **I — Interest**: Facts, story, or information that develops curiosity
- **D — Desire**: Show how this changes their life; feature → benefit → emotion
- **A — Action**: CTA — tell them exactly what to do

---

## 3. PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution)

Best for short-form copy: social media posts, email subject lines, short ads.

- **P — Problem**: Name the exact pain the reader has
- **A — Agitate**: Twist the knife — make the problem feel urgent and real
- **S — Solution**: Position your offer as the relief

**Example**:
> Struggling to get your LinkedIn posts seen? (Problem)
> You're spending hours writing and getting 3 likes. (Agitate)
> Here's the 5-step system that 3x'd my reach in 30 days. (Solution)

---

## 4. ACCA

Good for awareness-stage audiences who don't yet know they have a problem.

- **A — Awareness**: Make them aware the problem exists
- **C — Comprehension**: Help them understand what it means for them
- **C — Conviction**: Provide proof that your solution works
- **A — Action**: CTA

---

## 5. The 4 P's (Picture → Promise → Prove → Push)

Ideal for visual content, product posts, and feature announcements.

- **Picture**: Paint a vivid image of the outcome the reader wants
- **Promise**: Make the explicit promise that your product delivers it
- **Prove**: Evidence that backs up the promise
- **Push**: Urgency-driven CTA

## 6. The 4 U's (Headlines & Subject Lines)

Rate your headline on each (1 = weak, 4 = strong). Aim for 3/4 on at least 3.

| U | Question | Weak Example | Strong Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Urgent** | Is there a time element? | "Make more money" | "Make $3,000 extra this month" |
| **Unique** | Is it fresh? | "Save money on insurance" | "Why your insurance company hopes you never read this" |
| **Ultra-specific** | Numbers, names, precise details? | "Lose weight fast" | "Lose 11 lbs in 21 days without cutting carbs" |
| **Useful** | Clear benefit to the reader? | "New product announcement" | "The 3-minute trick that halves your email time" |

## 7. The BFD Formula (Prospect Psychology)

Use before writing anything to map your audience's core complex.

**Beliefs** — What does your audience believe about:
- The problem (is it real? is it their fault? can it be solved?)
- Your category (has it disappointed them before?)
- Your brand (unknown, trusted, skeptical?)

**Feelings** — Current emotional state:
- Fear: of loss, failure, embarrassment, being left behind
- Frustration: with current situation or past solutions
- Hope: what they want to believe is possible
- Desire: the dream outcome

**Desires** — What do they actually want?
- The end state, not the product (no one wants a drill; they want a hole)
- Write to the desire, use the product as the vehicle

**Application**: Write a 3-sentence BFD statement before every piece of copy:
> "My audience believes ___. They feel ___. They desperately want ___."

## 8. Features → Benefits Translation

Never stop at features. Always convert to benefits using the "So what? / Which means…" bridge.

| Feature | Bridge | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| "24/7 customer support" | Which means… | "Help whenever you need it — midnight panic included" |
| "AI-powered editing" | So what? | "Your posts look polished without hiring a copywriter" |
| "300+ templates" | Which means… | "Start writing in 30 seconds, not 3 hours" |
| "End-to-end encryption" | So what? | "Your conversations stay private — no one is reading your messages" |

Formula: **[Feature] + "which means" + [Benefit to reader]**

---

## 9. The Big Promise + Secondary Promise

Use when your main claim might sound too good to be true.

- **Big Promise**: The boldest, most compelling claim — the reason to act
- **Secondary Promise**: A smaller but more believable benefit that is *still* compelling on its own

If readers don't believe the big promise, they'll still act on the secondary promise.

**Example**:
> "This system can double your revenue in 90 days." (Big Promise)
> "And even if revenue stays flat, you'll cut 10 hours of work per week." (Secondary Promise)

---

## 10. The Bullet (Fascination) Formula

From Boardroom Publishing — the masters of ultra-specific, curiosity-driven bullets.

Formula: **Specific problem + mysterious/intriguing solution**
- Be specific about the **problem**
- Be vague and tantalizing about the **solution**

**Bad**: "Tips for sleeping better"
**Good**: "The $2 drug store item that knocks you out in 8 minutes — and it's not melatonin"

**Bad**: "How to reduce back pain"
**Good**: "How a pickpocket technique can cure chronic back pain"

## Quick Selection Guide

| Situation | Best Formula |
|---|---|
| Long-form post / sales page | Motivating Sequence |
| Short ad / caption | PAS |
| Product launch | 4 P's |
| Email / cold outreach | AIDA |
| New audience (no awareness) | ACCA |
| Headline/subject line review | 4 U's |
| Audience research | BFD |
| Writing product copy | Features → Benefits |
| High-skepticism offers | Big Promise + Secondary Promise |
| Bullet points / lists | Fascination Formula |
